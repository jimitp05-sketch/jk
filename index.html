Clinical Specialisations

Dr. Kothari Begins.

Advanced techniques fewer than 1 in 100 doctors are trained in — routinely applied at the bedside.

ECMO — Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation

When the heart and lungs can no longer sustain life independently, ECMO takes over. Dr. Kothari is among the rare intensivists in Gujarat trained to initiate, manage, and wean patients from this advanced external life-support circuit — reserved for the most severe cardiac and respiratory failure cases.

CRRT — Continuous Renal Replacement Therapy

Acute kidney injury in the ICU demands precision. Dr. Kothari employs CRRT — a 24/7 dialysis modality for haemodynamically unstable patients — to gently restore fluid and electrolyte balance while protecting other organ systems.

Sepsis & Septic Shock Management

Sepsis kills more patients than many cancers. Early recognition, targeted antibiotic therapy, and aggressive haemodynamic support are Dr. Kothari's tools of choice — applied with the Hour-1 Sepsis Bundle and decades of clinical pattern-matching.

Multi-Organ Failure & ARDS

When the body turns on itself — lungs stiffening, kidneys shutting, liver faltering simultaneously — the intensivist becomes the conductor. Dr. Kothari's structured approach to MODS and ARDS management has given families outcomes they were told were impossible.

Mechanical Ventilation & Weaning

Placing a patient on a ventilator is straightforward. Safely taking them off — without lung injury or ventilator-associated complications — is an art. Dr. Kothari's lung-protective strategies and systematic weaning protocols minimise ICU ventilator dependency.

Post-Surgical & High-Dependency ICU Care

The hours after major surgery are the most vulnerable. Dr. Kothari's critical care team provides seamless post-operative ICU management — monitoring haemodynamics, optimising analgesia, preventing complications, and ensuring the smoothest transition to recovery.

Illustrative clinical scenarios representing the types of critical care presentations managed at Apollo Hospitals ICU.

Sepsis & Shock

Septic Shock — Hour-1 Bundle Activation

A middle-aged patient presents with fever, hypotension, and confusion — meeting Sepsis-3 criteria. The ICU team initiates the SSC Hour-1 bundle: blood cultures, lactate measurement, broad-spectrum antibiotics, and vasopressor support. Daily SOFA scores guide progressive de-escalation.

ECMO / ARDS

Severe ARDS — VV-ECMO Protocol (EOLIA Criteria)

A patient with severe ARDS (PaO₂/FiO₂ <50 mmHg) is assessed for VV-ECMO per ELSO 2021 guidelines. After multidisciplinary team review, ECMO is initiated alongside lung-protective ventilation. Circuit management and daily weaning assessments are conducted by the ICU team.

AKI / CRRT

Post-Surgical AKI — CRRT Protocol (KDIGO 2022)

Following major cardiac surgery, a patient develops oliguric AKI with rising creatinine and haemodynamic instability. CRRT is initiated per KDIGO 2022 urgent indications. Continuous monitoring of fluid balance, electrolytes, and filter function allows precise, gentle renal support.

Common Questions

Answers Before You
Even Know to Ask.

The questions families ask most — answered clearly, without the jargon.

A critical care specialist — also called an intensivist — is a physician trained exclusively to manage life-threatening conditions. Unlike a general physician who coordinates care, an intensivist directly manages every system simultaneously: respiratory, cardiovascular, renal, neurological, and metabolic. Dr. Jay Kothari holds an FNB in Critical Care Medicine — one of India's most demanding super-specialisation qualifications — enabling him to manage cases where multiple systems are simultaneously failing.
ECMO (Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation) temporarily replaces the function of the heart and lungs — circulating blood outside the body, oxygenating it, and returning it — allowing the organs to rest and potentially recover. It is considered when a patient has severe respiratory or cardiac failure not responding to conventional ventilator and medication support. Dr. Kothari is one of the rare specialists in Gujarat trained to manage ECMO circuits.
Mechanical ventilation is a life-support measure, not a permanent state. When a patient cannot breathe adequately — due to infection, surgery, or organ failure — a ventilator breathes for them while the underlying cause is treated. The goal is always to wean the patient off the ventilator as safely and quickly as possible. Dr. Kothari's team uses lung-protective ventilation strategies that minimise complications and focus on gradual, systematic recovery.
