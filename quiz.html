Medical Myth Busters

Think you know the ICU? Tap a card to reveal the truth — some answers might surprise you.

👆 Tap any card to flip it
⚠️ MYTH
"If you're on a ventilator, you're in a coma and feel nothing."
Tap to reveal the truth →
✅ FACT
Many ventilated patients are conscious and aware. Sedation levels are carefully titrated — over-sedation increases ICU delirium, muscle weakness, and ICU length of stay. Light sedation protocols (like ABCDEF bundle) are now standard.
— Evidence-based ICU practice
⚠️ MYTH
"More IV fluids always help a shocked patient recover faster."
Tap to reveal the truth →
✅ FACT
Fluid overload is directly associated with increased ICU mortality. Modern sepsis protocols use dynamic fluid assessments (pulse pressure variation, PLR test) rather than aggressive volume loading. "De-resuscitation" is now a key ICU skill.
— SSC Guidelines 2021
⚠️ MYTH
"ECMO is a last resort — once on ECMO, patients rarely survive."
Tap to reveal the truth →
✅ FACT
With careful patient selection and experienced centres, VV-ECMO survival rates for severe ARDS reach 50–70%. The CESAR and EOLIA trials both demonstrated significant benefits. ECMO is a bridge to recovery, not a terminal decision.
— ELSO Registry Data 2023
⚠️ MYTH
"Sepsis only affects elderly or immunocompromised patients."
Tap to reveal the truth →
✅ FACT
Sepsis can affect anyone — from a 25-year-old with a dental infection to a healthy post-operative patient. It caused 11 million deaths in 2017 globally. Young, otherwise healthy people can deteriorate rapidly within hours of onset.
— Lancet Global Health 2020
⚠️ MYTH
"Once organs start failing, nothing more can be done."
Tap to reveal the truth →
✅ FACT
Modern ICU medicine can support multiple organ systems simultaneously — CRRT for kidneys, ECMO for heart/lungs, vasopressors for circulation, enteral nutrition for the gut. Many patients with 3-4 organ failure make full recoveries with expert ICU management.
— Multi-organ support therapy 2023
⚠️ MYTH
"Eating is not important in the ICU — patients are getting fluids."
Tap to reveal the truth →
✅ FACT
ICU nutrition is a life-saving therapy. Critically ill patients undergo hypermetabolism. Early enteral feeding (within 24–48hrs) reduces infections, ICU-acquired weakness, ventilation duration, and mortality. "Let food be thy medicine" applies most in the ICU.
— ESPEN ICU Guidelines 2023
⚠️ MYTH
"Higher doses of antibiotics are always better for severe infections."
Tap to reveal the truth →
✅ FACT
Antibiotic stewardship — using the right drug at the right dose for the right duration — is more effective than "more antibiotics." Overuse drives antimicrobial resistance, the next global health crisis. De-escalation once cultures return is critical ICU practice.
— WHO AMR Action Plan
⚠️ MYTH
"Once a patient leaves the ICU, they're fully recovered."
Tap to reveal the truth →
✅ FACT
Post-Intensive Care Syndrome (PICS) affects up to 50% of ICU survivors — causing physical weakness, cognitive decline, anxiety, depression, and PTSD for months to years. Discharge from ICU is the beginning, not the end, of the recovery journey.
— SCCM PICS Task Force

All facts are based on peer-reviewed evidence and current clinical guidelines. For medical decisions, always consult a qualified physician.

🫀 ECMO Facts

  • → ECMO was first used successfully in 1971 by Dr. Robert Bartlett
  • → A VV-ECMO circuit processes 5–7 litres of blood per minute outside the body
  • → ECMO centre volume directly correlates with survival — high-volume centres see 55–70% survival
  • → Only ~400 centres globally are certified ELSO ECMO centres

🦠 Sepsis Facts

  • → The Sepsis-3 definition (2016) replaced SIRS criteria — qSOFA is now the screening tool
  • → Lactate >2 mmol/L with suspected infection meets Sepsis-3 septic shock criteria
  • → India accounts for 10–12 million sepsis cases annually — largely under-diagnosed
  • → ICU-acquired infections affect up to 30% of admitted patients — prevention is critical

💨 Ventilation Facts

  • → Ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP) increases ICU stay by 8–10 days on average
  • → Prone positioning for 16+ hours/day reduces ARDS mortality by 16% (PROSEVA trial)
  • → Only 6 mL per kilogram of body weight — the lung-protective ventilation tidal volume target
  • → Daily spontaneous breathing trials (SBTs) help identify readiness for extubation

🌱 Post-ICU Facts

  • → PICS (Post-Intensive Care Syndrome) affects 30–50% of ICU survivors long-term
  • → ICU-acquired weakness (ICUAW) affects 25–50% of patients mechanically ventilated >7 days
  • → Early physiotherapy in the ICU (within 48 hours) dramatically reduces ICUAW incidence
  • → ICU diaries kept by nursing staff reduce PTSD in survivors by up to 35%
